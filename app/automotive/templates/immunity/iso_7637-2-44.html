ISO 7637 Part 2: Electrical transient conduction along supply lines only.

ISO 7637-2:2011 specifies test methods and procedures to ensure the compatibility to conducted electrical transients of equipment installed on passenger cars and commercial vehicles fitted with 12 V or 24 V electrical systems.

Equipment: Power supply (13/27V)! (battery), burst generator HL 0860 (Burst-Generator SFT 400 125 kHz), Wavetek 178 50MHz Waveform Generator, Managed Power Supply "Chroma" or "Elgar", Switch HL 2226
Pulse 1 Simulation of transients due to supply disconnection from inductive loads
Pulse 2a Simulates transients due to sudden interruption of currents in a device connected in parallel with the EUT due to the inductance of the wiring harness
Pulse 2b (The same setup as in test "Pulse 4") Simulates transients from DC motors acting as generators after the ignition is switched off.
Pulse 3a and 3b Simulation of transients, which occur as a result of the switching processes
Pulse 4 (The same setup as in test "Pulse 2b") The test pulses 4, 5a, and 5b have been removed from this edition of this part of ISO 7637, since they are specified in ISO 16750-2 and ISO 21848.

Pulse 1 - Negative Pulse from Parallel Inductive Load

A simulation of transients due to supply disconnection from inductive loads connected in parallel with the DUT, as used in the vicle.
500 pulses (if ISO 7637-2:2004 - 5000 pulses)

Pulse 1 with Va = 0 V is used to generate pulse 6.

 Verification
  • 2 Power supplies - for 13/27 V & 150/600 V
  • Wavetek 178 50MHz Waveform Generator
  • 2 specific automotive boxes for Pulse 1
  • 1 specific box as a load
  • Perform verification at 0V DC (Ua=0 V) - Increase the voltage on the power supply until the pulse reaches 150/600 V, implement the measurement of tr & td
  • Apply LV PS, and execute the calibration (Ua=13/27 V) - measurement t3, t2 & t1
  • Connect the DUT and save the result plot. Wait for the test to complete.
Wavetek generator configuration:
- set "Function" to "Square"
- set "Frequency" to 5 Hz
- set "Amplitude" to 12 V (up to 18 V), p-p
- set "Mode" to "Continue"
Connect Wavetek to the generator HL 2226
    Please, save the following plots:
  • Verification - Us, tr, td, t1, t2, t3
  • Verification under load (load instead of EUT) - Us, td
  • Calibration (without, but with Ua 13/27 V) - Us
  • EUT response - plot with multiple pulses, and plot with a single pulse

Pulse 4 Cold Crank Voltage Drop

The voltage reduction caused by energizing the starter motor circuits of the internal combustion engines

to edit

    Use the specific software to program pulse steps.
  • Swich on all the equipment in the cart
  • Open the "SWPS" software
  • Wait for GPIB connection to complete (If the software doesn't respond, physically delete all user sequences on the "ELGAR" device.)
  • Switch to the tab "...", and load software settings (e.g. "automotive_12_v.prg)
  • Switch to the tab "sequence", and load sequence config from HDD (algorithm: hdd->software->device RAM)
  • Set the program behave - loop, repeat, etc
  • (parallel mode must be disabled!) Make any changes and upload the config to SmartWave (ELGAR equipmant works with internal memory store only)
  • Switch on the "Output" button (tab "..." settings will be applied)
  • Switch on the "Sequence" (a window for selecting the uploaded configuration will be open)
  • Observe the process (The software doesn't contain any monitor system)

Aplicable for ISO 7637-2:2004(E) only

This pulse simulates supply voltage reduction caused by energizing the starter-motor circuits of internal combustion engines, excluding spikes associated with starting
